Program Reflection

Before applying to this program, I have been teaching high school physics for more than 20 years.Without any doubt, physics is one of the most challenging subjects at high school level. As the saying goes, necessity is the mother of invention and I was in need of making this challenging course more engaging to my students.Over all the years of teaching,I have experienced that the more I implement technology into my instruction, the more the students are engaged.

However, simply using technology tools in any unordered way would make instruction maybe a little more entertaining but not never effective by any means. This is the reason Learning Technologies and Design was the best match for my needs. I had the chance to learn how to put theories of design and instruction into practice in an academic way.

Personally, I have always been fascinated by the idea of design. I have always started my first day of school by discussing the scope of physics and how it deals with the beauty in nature. As Henri Poincaré, a pioneer scientist, said : “The scientist does not study nature because it is useful; he studies it because he delights in it, and he delights in it because it is beautiful. If nature were not beautiful, it would not be worth knowing, and if nature were not worth knowing, life would not be worth living.” Felix Mendelssohn defines beauty as ‘unity in variety’ which actually points to a design.Finally,Carl Sagan says “The beauty of a living thing is not the atoms that go into it, but the way those atoms are put together.” ; a minute change in the design of the atoms would end up with another substance.

This is true for knowledge and information as well! This program helped me to see the best practices of design in this sense. I have also learned how important aligning every assessment and learning activity of a course to the objectives is.
